Health informatics has moved from a niche academic field to a core function of every modern health organization. Here are the five skills that matter most right now.

## 1. Data Analytics and Statistical Reasoning

This is the foundation. Health informatics specialists need to be comfortable with statistical analysis — not just running tests, but understanding which test to use, when, and why.

## 2. Digital Health Platform Literacy

EHRs, telehealth platforms, remote patient monitoring systems — the modern health informatician needs to understand how these systems work and where the data gaps are.

## 3. Clinical Context and Domain Knowledge

The best health data analysts aren't just number crunchers — they understand the clinical context behind the data.

## 4. Data Visualization and Communication

An analysis that no one can understand is an analysis that no one uses. Health informatics specialists need to be skilled communicators.

## 5. Ethics, Privacy, and Equity

Health data is sensitive. The best informaticians ask proactive questions about whose data gets collected and who benefits from the analysis.

If you're hiring, look for candidates who can demonstrate all five. A great informatician is part analyst, part translator, part advocate.
